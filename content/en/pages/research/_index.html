The CIDS is committed to advancing research in the field of international dispute resolution, serving as a hub for scholarly inquiry and innovation. Its mission is to produce cutting-edge research that addresses contemporary challenges in arbitration and dispute settlement, contributing to both academic discourse and practical applications. By fostering interdisciplinary collaborations and engaging with leading experts, CIDS aims to generate impactful insights and develop new methodologies that enhance the effectiveness and fairness of international dispute resolution. The Center's research initiatives also strive to influence policy-making and improve the global legal framework governing international disputes.

notes: >- alt: >- credit: >- - kind: block template: chapter title: >- slug: >- ranks: self: 2 data: layout: no_background text: >-

Research in law, and in particular criteria for excellence in research, have over the last decades globally shifted from a service towards legal practice to a purpose in itself, seeking the advancement of knowledge and understanding. CIDS in part follows this trend but, aware of the importance of continued critical contributions by academia to the defense and development of pacific methods of dispute settlement, it also significantly engages in service to practice.
