Join us for a forward-looking multi-stakeholder discussion about the essential practical skills that arbitration practitioners need in order to thrive in a technology & AI-driven future.

Organized by the MIDS and hosted by the ICC, this panel aims to help equip the participants (which should be of particular interest to young / upcoming practitioners) with actionable insights to navigate the opportunities and challenges ahead.

The session will also offer a privileged opportunity to learn more and discuss informally about the MIDS program for candidates.

 Who is this event for?

MIDS Alumni, students, professors, as well as young arbitration practitioners are encouraged to join us for this interesting discussion at the ICC. Attendance is free of charge but registration is required.
  

Program

08:30 – 08:45 Welcome and introduction

  • Jacques de Werra, Professor, University of Geneva and MIDS Committee member
  • Ashleigh Brocchieri, Expert Counsel, ICC International Court of Arbitration

08:45 - 09:45 Panel
Digital technology in arbitration: How to prepare for the AI-driven future

  • Ashleigh Brocchieri, Expert Counsel, ICC International Court of Arbitration
  • Monica Crespo, Head of Product, Jus Mundi
  • Yuri Mantilla, Senior Associate, Freshfields Bruckhaus Deringer
  • Jacques de Werra, Professor, University of Geneva and MIDS Committee member
  • Moderator: Dilara Khamitova, International Arbitration Jurist, Clyde&Co

Location

ICC International Court of Arbitration
33-43 avenue du Président Wilson
75116 Paris
